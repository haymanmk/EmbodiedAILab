## Seminar Structure From Transcript

- 00:00-06:00 — motivation: end-to-end policy learning needs large data and struggles with human-like few-shot generalization across novel tasks and goals.
- 06:00-12:00 — central abstraction: model actions as compositional state/action concepts and constraints, not only direct observation-to-action policies.
- 12:00-18:00 — one-shot manipulation: contact analogy, DINOv2 visual correspondences, motion planning, grasping, and stability checks.
- 18:00-30:00 — spatial reasoning: LLMs or VLMs propose abstract spatial relationship graphs; compositional diffusion models ground those constraints into object poses.
- 30:00-39:00 — long-horizon manipulation: video-language models segment demonstrations; learned skill samplers and effect models are composed by planning over task skeletons.
- 39:00-48:00 — reflection: neuro-symbolic systems remain useful in the foundation-model era because they expose structure, modularity, and explicit composition rather than treating the robot system as one black-box model.
- 48:00-57:52 — Q&A: symbolic commonsense knowledge, how predicted future states are evaluated, why diffusion is used for multimodal trajectory/pose generation, preference utilities, closed-loop replanning, simulation, and the role of neural modules inside symbolic structure.
